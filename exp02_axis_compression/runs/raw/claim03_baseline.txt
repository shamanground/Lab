The statement "Increasing minimum wage always decreases employment" is **not supported by the majority of empirical economic research**.

**Key points:**

- **Empirical Evidence:** Numerous studies have examined the relationship between minimum wage increases and employment. Many find little to no significant negative effect on overall employment, especially for moderate increases and in low-wage sectors.
- **Not Always:** Some studies do find small negative effects, particularly for specific groups (e.g., teenagers), but others find neutral or even positive effects due to increased consumer spending.
